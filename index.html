TOLR Deep Dive · 08 — Gap to Par & the PGC Bridge
Two levers to close the recovery gap
TOLR's recovery gap vs TDMB/TRA isn't a single problem — it's two separable ones. Lever 1 (crosswalk): TOLR users already flow to TDMB/TRA, contributing cross-show revenue — this is already being captured and will grow. Lever 2 (same-show ARPPU): the % of revenue generated on TOLR itself is lower because content depth is shallower — this is what PGC content directly addresses. The benchmark isn't today's TDMB/TRA. It's TDMB in Jan 2025 and TRA in Jun 2025 — the same early-lifecycle stage TOLR is at now.

04 — Read-out

What the data shows
TOLR's D30 same-show ARPPU sits ~10–15pp below where TDMB was at Jan 2025 and ~12–18pp below TRA at Jun 2025. This gap persists through D90 and D180. The cause isn't acquisition quality — ARPU per payer is comparable. It's content depth: users convert, then drift to other shows faster because there's less TOLR content to hold them. Crosswalk captures much of that drift as cross-show revenue, but same-show stickiness is the remaining lever.
What PGC does
Each new PGC title added to TOLR raises the content surface — more hours for payers to consume on the same show before they drift. The target isn't to match current TDMB or TRA (which have far more content). It's to match where TDMB/TRA were at a comparable lifecycle stage. That's a realistic, near-term goal. Closing even half the D90 gap adds meaningfully to same-show recovery, compounding through D180 and beyond.
